Simple portion guide (no scales needed)
  • Protein (chicken/daal): your palm = 1 serving (~25g protein). Have 1 palm per meal.
  • Roti: you = 1 medium roti per meal. Husband = 2 rotis.
  • Rice: you = 1 small bowl (cooked, ~120g). Husband = 1.5–2 bowls.
  • Vegetables/sabzi: no limit — fill half your plate freely.

  • Tea/coffee: semi-skimmed milk kept as is — limit to 2 cups/day, use 1 sweetener tablet instead of sugar.
3 fat-loss rules that actually work
  • Protein first, every meal. It keeps you full, protects muscle, and requires more calories to digest.
  • Steps are your secret weapon. 8,000–10,000 steps/day = 300–400 extra kcal burned without cardio guilt.
  • Consistency beats perfection. One off-day doesn't ruin progress — getting back on track the next meal does.

Cardio strategy — the truth
  • Your 10–15 min cardio at end of sessions is enough — it depletes glycogen and burns fat
  • 8,000–10,000 steps/day burns an extra 300–400 kcal — more effective than forcing 30 min treadmill
  • Stair climber > flat treadmill for fat burning in short time
  • Incline walking (10–12% incline, 4–5 km/h) is your best friend — burns as much as running with zero joint stress
Progressive overload (every 2 weeks)
  • Weeks 1–2: learn form, moderate weight, finish all reps comfortably
  • Weeks 3–4: add 1–2 kg per exercise or 1 extra rep per set
  • Weeks 5–6: add another rep or another set
  • Every 6 weeks: take 1 deload week (lighter weights, same movements)

Batch Cook System
Cook Sunday + Wednesday. Eat 3–4 days from each batch. Mix and match to avoid boredom.
Sunday cook (2 hours, covers Mon–Thu)
  • Chicken: marinate and cook 800g–1kg chicken thighs in one pan. Store fridge in airtight container, lasts 4 days.
  • Daal: boil large pot of masoor or chana daal. Make tarka. Divide: half in fridge, half frozen in portions.
  • Rice: cook large batch plain rice. Fridge 4 days. Reheat with splash of water in microwave.
  • Roti: make a batch of 10–12, wrap in foil, fridge 3 days or freeze. Reheat in dry pan 1 min per side.
  • Veg: chop onion, tomato, cucumber ahead — store in fridge boxes for quick salads.
Wednesday top-up (30 min, covers Thu–Sat)
  • Fresh chicken batch or pull from freezer
  • Cook 1 sabzi (spinach/peas/aloo — 20 min)
  • Defrost daal portions from Sunday's batch
  • Make fresh roti or use frozen batch
Fridge & freezer plan
  • Fridge (4 days): cooked chicken, cooked rice, fresh daal portion, chopped salad veg, yoghurt, eggs
  • Freezer (1 month): daal in zip bags (portion sized), frozen roti stack, raw marinated chicken portions, cooked chicken pieces
  • Containers needed: 4–5 medium airtight containers (Lidl or Tesco £5–8 set). Label with day cooked.

Cravings & low energy management
  • Feeling angry or irritable: usually means blood sugar dipped — eat your snack, don't skip it. 1 banana or a few nuts solves this fast.
  • Sweet craving after dinner: 1 date + black tea OR 1 tsp honey in warm water. Satisfies without derailing progress.
  • Feeling very hungry at night: drink 1 glass water first, wait 10 min. If still hungry, eat 100g low-fat yoghurt — won't undo your day.
  • Low energy before gym: eat your snack 45 min before going. A banana or small roti + egg works well.
  • Weekend cravings: allow 1 small treat Sunday (1 biscuit, 1 piece of mithai) — planned indulgence prevents bingeing.
Menstrual Cycle Adjustments
Your cycle falls roughly 16th–20th each month. Adjust workouts and food accordingly — don't fight your body.
Days 1–15 (follicular + ovulation): Energy is higher, insulin sensitivity better. Push harder in gym — increase weights, higher intensity. Carbs absorb better here so eat normally or slightly more on gym days.
Days 16–20 (your period window): Reduce gym intensity — do lighter weights, more reps. No heavy compound lifts if you have cramps. Swap gym sessions for 30 min incline walking or yoga. Cravings are hormonal — allow 1 extra piece of fruit or 1 small sweet. Don't crash diet during this window — it increases hair loss risk.
Days 21–28 (luteal): Energy dips slightly, hunger increases. Prioritise protein and fibre to stay full. Don't add extra carbs — keep meals as normal. Good week to focus on steps and moderate workouts rather than PRs.
During period (Days 16–20) — gym swap plan
  • Replace heavy gym session with 30–40 min incline walk or stair climber at low intensity
  • Stretching or light yoga at home is valid exercise — don't skip movement entirely
  • Iron loss during period: eat lentils (daal), spinach, eggs — keep this in your rotation
  • Bloating is water retention, not fat gain — don't panic about scale during this week
  • Weigh yourself only once a week, on Wednesday morning after the bathroom, same conditions
